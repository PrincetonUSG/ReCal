Introducing ReCal.

Needle, out of the haystack.

Never sort through Blackboard, Piazza, email, and other notifications again.

ReCal keeps you on track with your course work. It's all in one place, and curated by your classmates.

ReCal is the calendar designed just for you. With a group calendar for every class, now you can collaborate with your classmates friends to stay on track with your work.

Now open for FREE to all Princeton students!

What is ReCal?

ReCal — short for Rethinking Calendar — is a new, crowdsourced way for Princeton students to monitor their assignments and other course work.

Start by signing up for classes. We'll generate a real-time, up-to-date dashboard for you with everything that's going on.

No more need to comb through a large webpage to find a certain preceptor's office hours or an assignment's due date. It's all right here in ReCal, handily curated by your classmates.

Earn points for good edits

Every part of ReCal has been designed to enable good content. See an error in an event? To edit, just click on the error, and type away.

In fact, we'll reward you with points for your edits. As soon as your classmates up-vote your changes, you'll earn a nice share of points — and so will they.

But beware! You lose points for submitting something that many of your classmates think is wrong.

Add an event first to get the most points!

Noticed that an event for a class is missing from ReCal? Adding it is a click away — and your friends get to benefit, too.

Your Agenda on the go.

Go mobile. Load www.recal.io in your smartphone's browser to see your Agenda from anywhere.

Or install our Chrome Extension. It spruces up your New Tab page with what's new on your Agenda.

Fully customizable. Position your events, your way.

ReCal is your calendar — it will look exactly the way you want. Want to see two events side by side? No problem, just drag and drop them side-by-side, and you're good to go.

We know Princeton students don't have the time to hunt for things in their full calendars. So we came up with the Agenda view to filter away the noise.
Decide what's most important to you — like assignments and exams but not lectures — and we'll show only those items in your Agenda.

Looks great in black.

Speaking of looks, there is a dark theme, too. Choose whichever fits you more.
